# Atech Arcade — Speaker Script

Open `pitch/index.html` in a browser (←/→ to advance, **P** to print to PDF). Timed
for ~4 minutes; one block per slide. Say the honesty line out loud on slide 8 — it
builds trust and it's still a winning story.

---

**① Title — 0:00**
> "This is Atech Arcade. It's a retro game *console* — and an ambient pixel display — and it clicks together from stock Atech modules with zero solder. It's the flagship that sells your kit."

**② The opportunity — 0:15**
> "Atech's kit can build *anything*. That's also the hard part — 'anything' is hard to sell. Meanwhile retro is having a real moment: Playdate sells out, the mini-consoles are back, pixel art is everywhere. So we built the one thing that makes the kit obvious in a glance — and remember, this is Build-for-the-Market, and *you* are the market. We built a product line for the people holding the scorecard."

**③ The product — 0:50**
> "Two boards snap together. The brain: a Motherboard with a color scoreboard, a speaker, and two click-in knobs with LED rings — it runs the OS and every game. The screen: twelve Light Grid tiles making a 108-pixel matrix that just glows. On top is an on-device OS with ten games and ambient modes. Play Pong, Snake, egg-catch, a jukebox — and when you stop, it becomes a clock or a drifting pixel scene. One purchase, two reasons to keep it on the desk."

**④ Platform, not a toy — 1:25**
> "This isn't one hacked game — it's a platform. Every game is dependency-free C++ that runs on the firmware *and* in a desktop simulator, so the whole library is testable off-hardware. Five themes restyle the entire console from design tokens. Clean ports-and-adapters seams mean adding a game is one class. And there's a clear blade for later: an installable game market."

**⑤ The market — 1:55**
> "Here's the wedge. A two-hundred-twenty-nine-dollar Playdate plays alone. A two-hundred-dollar Tidbyt just sits there. Atech Arcade is two-forty-nine and does *both*. Everyone on this list is either a player or a display — we're the only credible *both*. That's what earns a Playdate-tier price with Tidbyt-tier everyday utility. And to be fair: a fifty-dollar pixel clock already runs toy games, so we don't win on 'it has games' — we win on console-grade play, real controls, and the Atech kit underneath."

**⑥ The business — 2:30**
> "It's a hardware razor with a software blade. A ladder from a thirty-nine-dollar software SKU up to the two-forty-nine Duo. The Duo's parts cost about two-ninety-two at *your retail* — twelve Light Grids alone are a hundred and eight — so nobody else can build it for two-forty-nine. At your internal cost it's one-twenty to one-seventy-five: thirty to fifty-two percent gross margin. Then the Pass and game packs keep earning at ninety-five percent, forever."

**⑦ Why Atech wins — 3:05**
> "The real prize is pull-through. Every single Duo moves five module types — and *twelve* Light Grids, the SKU nobody otherwise buys twelve of. It opens gamer, gift, and classroom shelves for the same modules, adds recurring content revenue on rails you already own, and needs no new hardware. And the economics only close as a first-party Atech product — which is the moat. This is uniquely yours to own."

**⑧ Honest status — 3:35** *(say it out loud)*
> "Straight with you: the *full* console — the OS and all ten games — runs on the real hardware today; the brain streams frames to the screen board and we've verified it end to end at about sixty frames a second. The board-to-board link is a laptop USB bridge for now — native wireless is written and in-tree, just not switched on for a noisy demo room. The game market is roadmap. It's a working prototype and credible economics, not a finished retail unit — and it's still a business."

**⑨ The ask — 3:55**
> "So the ask is simple: adopt Atech Arcade as your flagship build-it-yourself console and ambient display. We bring the working build and the model; you bring the catalog, the cost base, and the storefront. Powered by Atech."
